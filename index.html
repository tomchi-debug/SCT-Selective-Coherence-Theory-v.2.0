Abstract

We derive, within the framework of Selective Coherence Theory (SCT), that the coherence field profile around any massive source decays as C(r) ~ r−D where D = d − σ in d spatial dimensions, and σ is the exponent of the non-local coupling kernel. This result follows rigorously from the Green's function of the SCT equation in the linear regime. In three spatial dimensions, the observed galaxy two-point correlation exponent γ ≈ 1.77 constrains σ = 1.23. We document six independent physical contexts — spanning twenty-five orders of magnitude in length scale — in which D = 1.77 appears, and conjecture D = √π ≈ 1.7725. Three new experimental predictions follow, all sharing spectral index −2/D = −1.13 — zero free parameters.
